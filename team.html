Why it's built this way

Why the partnership matters.

An incubator is a promise: bring us your idea and we'll get you to launch. That promise is only as good as the people behind it. Here's why this structure can keep it.

01 — INFRASTRUCTURE

The hard part is already running.

Keeping subnets alive 24/7 is the unglamorous work most teams underestimate. Rizzo already does it at scale — so incubated teams build on a foundation that exists, not a promise.

02 — PIPELINE

Good intakes start with good sourcing.

An incubator is only as strong as the teams it admits. A dedicated sourcing partner means intakes are filled from a real pipeline — not whoever happens to find the form.

03 — JUDGMENT

Experience that compounds.

Advisory depth across the partnership means teams get more than money and servers — they get the judgment that turns a working prototype into a subnet that survives.

How it's designed

Designed so the whole ecosystem compounds.

SN20's flywheel was engineered on one principle: every participant gains at the same time. The subnet, its token holders, its crowd funders, and its incubation teams all grow together — and so does Bittensor, with each graduate adding a proven new subnet to the network. It's an alignment you can trace yourself on the flywheel page.
